Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is an environmentally friendly approach to managing pests that combines multiple methods to reduce reliance on chemical pesticides.

What is Integrated Pest Management?

IPM focuses on long-term prevention of pests or their damage by managing the ecosystem. It combines biological control, habitat manipulation, and use of resistant varieties to reduce pest populations.

Steps to Implement IPM

  1. Monitor pest populations using traps and field observations.
  2. Identify pests and beneficial insects accurately.
  3. Use biological controls, such as introducing natural predators or parasites.
  4. Apply chemical pesticides only when necessary, and choose targeted, low-toxicity options.
  5. Evaluate the success of pest management strategies regularly.
